Lesson One: Important rules

There are three steps to making a word:

1. Get the root word.

2. Find the part of speech, gender, and subtype of the word.

3. Find the correct ending for the word based on these three things.

Example: the sentence 'I breathe'.

1. Find the roots. (I=mi, and air=e)

2. Find the POS, gender, and subtype. (mi=pronoun, and it does not have specific gender (neutral/3rd declension) or subtype that is necessary. e is air when it is a noun, but when it is a verb is breathe, and it needs to have the neutral gender ending for the subtype 'Present 3rd declension (Neutral Gender) Singular'.

3. Find the word's endings (mi is a pronoun that does not need endings, and e needs the ending -tia for Present 3rd Declension (Neutral) Singular.)

Our completed sentence: mi etia.

Other rules:

Shmorsh has the english alphabet but there are NO SILENT LETTERS!

Shmorsh language order is the same as english.

Though Shmorsh may seem like a worse version of English, it is also based on Latin! (As you can tell because of all the word endings we added - you will see in future lessons!)
